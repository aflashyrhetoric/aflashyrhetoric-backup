kevin oh | about

Six years ago, a family friend showed me her new website. "I got it done professionally," she said.

I saw the site and cringed.

It had obnoxious electric-blue banners and indecipherable fonts. Things were scrolling and flashing all over the place. In the corner, I spotted a stolen watermarked stock photo. I wasn't a professional, but I knew this wasn't good work.

She had paid nearly $800 for this steaming pile of website.

It was a crime. I decided to learn HTML & CSS and re-did her site for free. Granted, it wasn't amazing, but it was something.

She smiled when I showed her the revision. I was hooked.

Since then, web design has become a bit of an obsession of mine. I researched modern design standards, seeking to improve my process in every possible way. I'd obsess about text editors (Sublime all the way!), responsive layouts, front-end/back-end frameworks, private VPS servers, all that jazz.

I learned quite a bit about what a website can be: anything from a personal journal to a public portfolio; a simple online business card to a primary source of income.

I've also learned that many wonderful people tend to compromise for cheaper lower-quality sites, operating under the impression that a proper website will break the bank.

I'm here to dispel this notion.

Ubiquitous, accessible, and affordable. Websites have become the way to build a solid, unique identity to show the world.

I'm passionate about what I do.

I believe in bold designs that don't sacrifice functionality. I believe that honest conversations are the best way to producing great work.

If you're interested in working with me, please don't hesitate to contact me here. Thanks.
